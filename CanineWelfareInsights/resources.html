Curated References

Below are the references that provide further insights into canine welfare. These sources include foundational frameworks, scientific studies, and reputable guidelines in animal welfare.

  • Farm Animal Welfare Council. (1979). Five Freedoms [Concept defining basic animal welfare standards].
  • Hiby, E. F., Rooney, N. J., & Bradshaw, J. W. S. (2004). Dog training methods: their use, effectiveness and interaction with behaviour and welfare. Animal Welfare, 13(1), 63–69.
  • Beerda, B., Schilder, M. B. H., van Hooff, J. A., de Vries, H. W., & Mol, J. A. (1998). Behavioural, saliva cortisol and heart rate responses to different types of stimuli in dogs. Applied Animal Behaviour Science, 58(3–4), 365–381.
  • Casey, R. A., Loftus, B., Bolster, C., Richards, G. J., & Blackwell, E. J. (2014). Human-directed aggression in domestic dogs (Canis familiaris): Occurrence in different contexts and risk factors. Applied Animal Behaviour Science, 152, 52–63.
  • ASPCA. (n.d.). General Dog Care – Dog Care Tips. Retrieved from aspca.org/pet-care/dog-care.
  • ASPCA. (n.d.). Position Statement on Responsibilities of Animal Breeders. Retrieved from aspca.org.
  • Coppola, C. L., Grandin, T., & Enns, R. M. (2006). Human interaction and cortisol: Can human contact reduce stress for shelter dogs? Physiology & Behavior, 89(3), 537–541.
  • Todd, Z. (2020). Wag: The Science of Making Your Dog Happy. Greystone Books.
  • Association of Shelter Veterinarians. (2010). Guidelines for Standards of Care in Animal Shelters.
